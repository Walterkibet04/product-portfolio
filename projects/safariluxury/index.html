Safari Luxury
Reservation web application

Try the prototype
Mobile first web application designed to help users or guests to book hotels and luxury tents for a stay.
Case Study
Problem at hand

How can i design a mobile first hotel or tent reservation website.

Project Overview

This is a mobile first tent or hotel reservation website that enables users to browse an see the rooms/tents available, specific details and book in few simple steps. The website designed with both travelers and service providers in mind, giving guests a smooth booking experience.

Goal

The goal was to create a user-friendly interface that reduces booking friction, improves trust through clear information and visuals, and ensures accessibility across devices.

Research

Research and discovery

I focused my research on people who travel most of the time and those who like touring places.

Define

Research Insights

The research findings showed that:
1. Most of the hotels don't have online booking process, instead you have to make a phone call.
2. Reviews and testimonials increase confidence in booking.
3. Some of the hotels have complicated booking process.
4. Guests value clear information on amenities and inclusions.
5. Mobile-first experience is critical for the travelers.

Persona
User Journey map
Define & Ideation

Design approach

With these research findings in mind, I aimed to design an website that is simple to use and useful embracing mobile first approach.



Prototype

Since stakeholders were not interested in wireframes, i jumped straight into mockups and interactive protoype to bring the ideas to life. The goal was to test the core features of the website which is booking or reservation. This prototype allowed me to see how real users interacted with the website and spot areas for improvement early.



Final product

The final product allows users to easily browse available tents, select the tent of their choice, view details and book for a stay. The website also has core features like progress indicators during the booking process.

Reflection

What Iâ€™ve learned

I learned that a luxury website should not only look beautiful but also be easy to use. Clear prices and simple policies make people trust the service more and feel safe to book. I also noticed how important mobile design is, since most travelers use their phones to make reservations. Progress indicators when booking, good photos and honest reviews turned out to be very powerful in helping people decide and feel confident about their stay.
