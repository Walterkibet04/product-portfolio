Currency exchange
Mobile app

Try the prototype
A fintech platform that empowers people to receive, send, exchange, and manage multiple currencies effortlessly, while offering free international bank accounts.
Problem at hand

African students living abroad often face challenges when trying to exchange currency quickly, affordably, and securely. Traditional financial institutions and currency exchange services typically charge high fees, have unfavorable exchange rates, and involve long wait times or tiresome processes.

Project Overview

Exchanging currencies can be frustrating for students abroad and people who have moved into a new country. AfriBDC is a Canada based fintech platform that helps solve this by giving users multi currency wallets or foreign accounts, Peer to peer exchange and direct exchange or swap at a fair rates. I worked on designing AfriBDC currency exchange app seamless and suit the needs of users. I learned alot being a sole designer owning the entire design process from User research → userflows → Wireframes → Prototype → user testing. It was challenging designing multi currency wallets and P2P exchange but I learned alot simplifying fintech experience.

Goal

Help African students abroad to exchange currencies and send currencies to loved ones at home. Simplify the currency exchange process by eliminating intermediaries, offering competitive rates, and ensuring faster transactions. Also to Simplify complex actions like currency conversion and wallet funding, so I focused on clarity, hierarchy, and intuitive navigation.

Research

Research and discovery

I focused my research on students abroad and those living in a foreign country and many shared almost same frustrations: no being able to easily exchange currencies, send money, pay for goods and services, and managing invoice in one app, struggling to open local bank acounts limited access to different currency wallets. I also reviewed other fintech apps to better understand the industry mos of them were complex and didn't provide Peer to peer exchange and virtual cards for payment.

Define

Research Insights

The research findings showed that:
1. Handling bills, expenses, and payments across many apps is confusing and time-consuming.
2. Limited access to global currency accounts – Hard for users to open and manage accounts in different currencies.
3. High fees & poor exchange rates.
4. Other apps are complex and don’t offer P2P exchange or virtual cards.

Persona
Define & Ideation

Design approach

With these research findings in mind, I aimed to design an app that was simple, usable, equitable enjoyable, and useful. My process started with mapping out user flows for key actions like sending money, exchanging currencies, and creating virtual cards for payment. From there, I created wireframes and prototypes to test how easily users could complete these tasks.

Wireframes sample

Before jumping into the visual design, I created low-fidelity wireframes to map out the app’s structure and main user flows.



Prototype

I created an interactive prototype to bring the ideas to life. The goal was to test the core features like sending money, exchanging currencies, and creating virtual cards for payment.. This prototype allowed me to see how real users interacted with the app and spot areas for improvement early on.



Testing

While testing the app, some users said that they wanted to filter P2P exchange request posts based on currency and exchange rates they are interested in.


Refine

So to solve this I added filter button on P2P posts list page where when the user clicks on it a filter modal will open and the user will be able to filter using different parameters such as currency, exchange rate and time posted and apply the filters.

Business consideration

Balancing User needs and Business goals

Users wanted a chat section in order to be able to negotiate Peer to Peer exchange with other users, to implement this, I added a chat section in the app. To prevent users from exchanging contacts and negotiaing outside the platform, i had to design it in way that users will select pre-defined messages and when they want to type exchange amount developers implemented it in a way that they can't type digits exceeding exchange amount posted also they can't type 10 digits which is equivalent to phone number.

Final product

The final product allows users to easily exchange currencies, receive, send money access to multi-currency wallets . With features like different currencies accounts, P2P exchange, and virtual cards, users can manage everything from payments to expenses in one place. The design is simple and intuitive, making complex financial tasks funding wallets easier.

Reflection

What I’ve learned

As the company grows, there will be some iterations like, improving existing features, refining flows, and adding new solutions as user needs evolve. The most important lesson I learnt from this project is constantly asking for feedback from the users to be able to reveal the gap in the design. Especially in a start-up environment as a sole designer. Which has helped me grow as a designer, taking ownership of the entire design process.
