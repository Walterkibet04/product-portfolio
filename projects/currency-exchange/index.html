Currency exchange
Mobile app

Try the prototype
A fintech platform that empowers people to receive, send, exchange, and manage multiple currencies effortlessly, while offering free international bank accounts.
Problem at hand

Users often struggle to exchange currency in a way that is fast, affordable, and secure. Traditional financial institutions and currency exchange services frequently impose high fees, provide poor exchange rates, and require lengthy or cumbersome processes.

Project Overview

Exchanging currencies can be frustrating for many users due to high fees, poor exchange rates, and complex processes. AfriBDC is a Canada-based fintech platform that addresses these challenges by offering multi-currency wallets (foreign accounts), peer-to-peer exchanges, direct currency swaps, sending money at fair rates. I worked on designing the AfriBDC currency exchange app to create a seamless experience tailored to user needs. As the sole designer, I owned the entire design process—from user research and user flows to wireframes, prototypes, and usability testing. Designing multi-currency wallets and P2P exchange flows was particularly challenging, but it strengthened my ability to simplify complex fintech experiences.

Goal

The goal was to design a fintech platform that allows users to:

1. Hold and manage multiple currency wallets
2. Exchange currencies through direct exchange or peer-to-peer matching.
3. Send and receive money across borders seamlessly.
4. Create virtual cards
5. Complete complex financial tasks with confidence and clarity

A key design principle was simplicity—making advanced financial actions feel approachable, even for non-technical users.

Research

Research and discovery

I focused my research on students abroad and those living in a foreign country and many shared almost same frustrations: no being able to easily exchange currencies, send money, pay for goods and services, and managing invoice in one app, struggling to open local bank acounts limited access to different currency wallets. I also reviewed other fintech apps to better understand the industry mos of them were complex and didn't provide Peer to peer exchange and virtual cards for payment.

Define

Research Insights

The research findings showed that:
1. Handling bills, expenses, and payments across many apps is confusing and time-consuming.
2. Limited access to global currency accounts – Hard for users to open and manage accounts in different currencies.
3. High fees & poor exchange rates.
4. Trust is a major concern when dealing with P2P exchanges.
5. Other apps are complex and don’t offer P2P exchange or virtual cards.

Persona
Define & Ideation

Design approach

With these research findings in mind, I aimed to design an app that was simple, usable, equitable enjoyable, and useful. My process started with mapping out user flows for key actions like wallet funding, sending money, exchanging currencies, and creating virtual cards for payment. From there, I created low-fidelity wireframes and prototypes to test how easily users could complete these tasks.

Wireframes sample


Prototype

I created an interactive prototype that demonstrates the app functionality and can be used for testing. The goal was to test the core features like funding wallet, sending money, exchanging currencies, and creating virtual cards for payment.. This prototype allowed me to see how real users interacted with the app and spot areas for improvement early on.



Testing

While testing the app, some users said that they wanted to filter P2P exchange request posts based on currency and exchange rates they are interested in.


Refine

So to solve this I added filter button on P2P posts list page where when the user clicks on it a filter modal will open and the user will be able to filter using different parameters such as currency, exchange rate and time posted and apply the filters.


Key Changes Made

1. Added filters to P2P exchange listings (currency, rate, amount)
2. Simplified the exchange confirmation flow.
3. Introduced predefined chat messages to prevent off-platform negotiations.
4. Improved visibility of exchange rates and balances

Business consideration

Balancing User needs and Business goals

Users wanted a chat section in order to be able to negotiate Peer to Peer exchange with other users, to implement this, I added a chat section in the app. To prevent users from exchanging contacts and negotiaing outside the platform, i had to design it in way that users will select pre-defined messages and when they want to type exchange amount developers implemented it in a way that they can't type digits exceeding exchange amount posted also they can't type 10 digits which is equivalent to phone number.

Developer Handoff

Developer Handoff

To ensure a smooth transition from design to development, I prepared:
1. Design system: colors, typography, spacing, and components
2. Component states: empty, loading, success, and error states.
3. User flow documentation for key features.
4. Annotated screens explaining interactions and edge cases.
5. Responsive behavior notes for different screen sizes.

All designs were structured for easy reference in design tools, enabling developers to implement the product efficiently and accurately.

Final product

The final product allows users to easily exchange currencies, receive, send money access to multi-currency wallets . With features like different currencies accounts, P2P exchange, and virtual cards, users can manage everything from payments to expenses in one place. The design is simple and intuitive, making complex financial tasks funding wallets easier.

Reflection

What I’ve learned

As the company grows, there will be some iterations like, improving existing features, refining flows, and adding new solutions as user needs evolve. The most important lesson I learnt from this project is constantly asking for feedback from the users to be able to reveal the gap in the design. Especially in a start-up environment as a sole designer. Which has helped me grow as a designer, taking ownership of the entire design process.
