E-learning
mobile app

Try the prototype
Learning application that aims to provide seamless and engaging learning experience to individuals of all ages and backgrounds.
Problem at hand

Traditional education materials such as textbooks maybe old or lacking interaction. Difficult mobility in different learning resources can frustrate users amking hard for them to complete courses.

Project Overview

My Role - Product Designer

With the surge in online education, many learners struggle to find mobile platforms that are both easy to use and adaptable to their skill level. This app set out to change that—offering instant access to courses, interactive exercises, and educational resources, all from a smartphone. My role was to transform the mobile app experience into a seamless, intuitive journey for learners of all backgrounds. From simplifying navigation to refining visual hierarchy, I ensured every interaction felt effortless, helping learners focus on what truly matters: learning. I learned lots being the sole designer owning all aspects of the design from userflows → Wireframes → Prototype → QA. It was challenging but fun working with a team of software devs to create a friendlier mobile experience.

Goals

Design the learning app to be user friendly by providing clear navigation and offering learning materials such as videos, notes, and pdf, empowering users to achieve their learning goals efficiently.

Discovery and research

Before diving into design, I wanted to understand what learners truly needed from a mobile learning platform. I started by reviewing competitor apps to see what worked well and where they fell short. I then conducted short interviews with learners from different backgrounds—some were complete beginners, others were already comfortable with online courses. This helped me uncover key pain points, such as confusing navigation, cluttered layouts, and a lack of progress tracking. I also gathered feedback on what motivated them to keep learning, which guided many of the design decisions later on. By the end of this phase, I had a clear picture of the features that must be included on this app.

Define

Research Insights

The research findings showed that:
1. They don’t have much free time, so short lessons they can finish in one sitting work best.
2. Seeing their progress keeps them motivated to continue.
3. If the app is hard to navigate, they lose interest quickly.
4. They prefer practical skills they can use right away over long theory-based content.

Persona
Define & Ideation

Design approach

With these research findings in mind, I aimed to design an app that was simple, flexible, and motivating. The layout is clean, lessons are easy to find, and progress tracking is front and center. This way, learners can open the app anytime, do a quick lesson, and feel like they’re making real progress without feeling overwhelmed.

Wireframes sample

Before jumping into the visual design, I created low-fidelity wireframes to map out the app’s structure and main user flows. This helped me focus on the layout, content placement, and navigation without getting distracted by colors or typography.



Prototype

I created an interactive prototype to bring the ideas to life. The goal was to test the core features—simple navigation, quick lesson access, and visible progress tracking—before moving into final design. This prototype allowed me to see how real users interacted with the app and spot areas for improvement early on.

Testing

While testing the app, some learners said long lessons felt overwhelming, certificates weren’t easy to find after completion, and the chat section was a bit hidden. A few also wanted quicker ways to ask questions while learning.


Refine

I added a certificate preview and download option inside the course section, so learners could see their achievement as soon as they finished. I added a clearly visible chat button at the bottom navigation.

final product

The final app offers a simple, engaging learning experience for university students and graduates. Lessons are easy to follow, certificates are available right inside each course, and the chat feature makes it quick to get help or ask questions. Everything is designed to keep learners motivated and make learning possible anytime, anywhere.

Reflection

What I learned

This project has taught me the value of listening closely to the users. Small changes—like moving a button or breaking lessons into shorter parts—can have a big impact on how people experience an app. I also learned the importance of testing early and often, so ideas can be improved before they become final.
