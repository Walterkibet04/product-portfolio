TeleHealth

Try the prototype
A Telehealth Platform Designed for Dignity, Simplicity, and Independence
Case Study
Problem at hand

How can I design a mobile telehealth experience that allows elderly users to easily consult a doctor, while also giving them simple, non-overwhelming access to their health records and medications?.

Project Overview

The elderly population is growing rapidly, increasing the demand for remote healthcare services. While telehealth platforms exist, many elderly users struggle to adopt them due to complex interfaces, small text, and overwhelming medical information. In addition to virtual consultations, elderly patients often need help managing ongoing health information such as medications, blood pressure, weight, and temperature. Poorly designed health records can increase confusion and anxiety rather than provide support.

Goal

The primary goal was to design a telehealth mobile app that enables elderly users to:
1. Book and attend a virtual consultation independently.
2. Feel confident and reassured while using the app.
3. View key health information (vitals and medications) without cognitive overload.



Success Criteria

1. Users can book an appointment in a few simple steps.
2. Users can join a video consultation with one tap.
3. Users can clearly understand their medications and recent health records.
4. Users do not feel overwhelmed by medical data.

Research

Research and discovery

I focused my research on elderly adults age 60+ who have limited tech sarviness, may have visual, hearing, or motor impairments, often managing chronic conditions requiring regular medication.

Define

Research Insights(User needs)

1. Clear navigation and large touch targets.
2. Easy access to medication instructions and recent health records.
3. High contrast user interface.
4. Simple language instead of complex medical terms.

Market & Competitive Analysis
Persona
User Journey map

1. Open the app.
2. Book an appointment.
3. Receive confirmation.
4. Join the video consultation.
5. Review consultation summary.
5. View medications and recent health records.




Design Tradeoffs & Rationale
Define & Ideation

Design approach

With these research findings in mind, I aimed to design an app that is simple to use and useful.



Prototype

I built mockups and interactive protoype to bring the ideas to life and conducted usability tests the goal was to test the core features of the app which is booking an appointment, viewing medications and recent health records. This prototype allowed me to see how real users interacted with the app and spot areas for improvement early.



Final product

The final product allows users to easily browse doctors, select date and time of their choice and book appointment, view health records, medication and also join video call with the doctor.
